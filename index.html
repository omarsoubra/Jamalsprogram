CARDIO PROTOCOL

Your concrete job already gives you ~6 hours of physical work a day — that IS your cardio. The protocol below is light, recovery-focused, and exists to support hypertrophy, not compete with it.

20–30 minutes on rest days, if you feel like it
Easy pace — phone off, no music required, just movement

Skip if you're wrecked from concrete work. Your job is the cardio. This is for general health and headspace.
Weeks 5–8 · Build
Same — Recovery Walk
20–30 minutes on rest days, optional
Easy pace, ideally outdoors

Phase 2 surplus is the biggest of the program — recovery is the bottleneck. Don't add cardio that competes with growth. Your concrete work covers the bases.

Optional Walk + Mobility
20–30 minutes walk, plus 10 min hip/ankle mobility
Surplus is dialed back, training intensity is at peak

If you want to add anything, make it mobility work for legs (your concreting is brutal on hips and ankles). Save energy for the heavy lifts.

NUTRITION

Science-based phased nutrition. Calories drop each block as your body adapts. Every meal, every day, every week — all 12 weeks mapped out.

THE SCIENCE
TDEE ~2,750 kcal (active labor + training). Protein set at 2.5g/kg (~170g/day) per Morton et al. (2018) for hypertrophy. Surplus increases through Phase 2 to drive growth, then dials back in Phase 3 to keep you lean. Carbs concentrated around training; pre-bed casein/cottage cheese supports overnight muscle protein synthesis on short-sleep nights.

The Other Stuff

LIFESTYLE

Training and food are 70% of the equation. The rest comes down to sleep, stress, and staying consistent when motivation dips.

😴
SLEEP
Target 7+ hours every night. You're averaging ~6 — this is the single biggest leverage point you have. Leproult & Van Cauter (2011) showed 1 week of 5-hr sleep cut testosterone 10–15%, and Dattilo et al. (2011) showed sleep restriction blunts muscle protein synthesis. A 1-hour sleep increase is worth more than any program tweak we could make.

  • Set a consistent bedtime — same time every night
  • No screens 30 mins before bed if possible
  • Magnesium Glycinate before bed will genuinely help
  • Keep your room cool and dark
🧠
STRESS
High stress = elevated cortisol = blunted muscle protein synthesis and easier abdominal fat storage. You said stress runs high — we can't make your life less stressful, but we can blunt the cortisol exposure with a few small habits.

  • Training itself is one of the best stress relief tools — use it
  • Five quiet minutes off your phone — even just sitting still — drops acute cortisol
  • 10 mins of deep breathing or a walk after work makes a difference
  • Don't skip rest days — they matter
📸
TRACK PROGRESS
The scale doesn't tell the whole story. Track these too:

  • How your clothes fit (especially waist)
  • Strength improvements week to week
  • Energy levels throughout the day
  • Weekly photos — same lighting, same pose, front and side
  • Sleep quality out of 10
📅

  • Weigh yourself — morning, after toilet, before food
  • Take front and side photo in the same spot
  • Rate energy, sleep, and stress out of 10
  • Log it in the Tracker tab
